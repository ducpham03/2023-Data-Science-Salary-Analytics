Welcome to the Data Science Salary Analytics
A Project by Duc Pham

My project is dedicated to providing an extensive and detailed visualization of salary distributions within various job categories in the field of data science. By highlighting how salaries vary by different sub-fields in Data Science categories and experience levels, this project is designed to aid both existing data professionals and those aspiring to enter the field by offering critical insights that support informed career decisions.

Through below visualizations, visitors can navigate the complex landscape of data science careers, gaining a deeper understanding of the financial and professional opportunities available in various specializations.

This interactive bar chart showcases the top five data science categories with the highest average salaries, directly aligning with the project's goal of highlighting the most lucrative areas within the field.

Users can easily compare these top categories and also select additional categories from the controls provided to visualize and compare their average salaries. This feature allows for a comprehensive understanding of salary structures across different sectors in data science, aiding in informed career decision-making.

Machine Learning and AI command the highest salaries in data science, with earnings that double those in Data Management. This significant disparity underscores the premium placed on advanced analytical skills and the capacity to innovate with complex algorithms, crucial for driving cutting-edge technologies and solutions.

This boxplot provides an insightful visualization of the salary distributions within different data science categories, serving as a crucial tool for comparing salaries across sub-fields. Unlike simple averages, the boxplot reveals a fuller picture of the data distribution, including the median, quartiles, and the presence of any outliers, which helps us understand the spread and central tendencies of salaries.

By facilitating a deeper understanding of the range and distribution of salaries, rather than just the average, the boxplot enables stakeholders—ranging from job seekers to corporate decision-makers—to make more informed decisions that consider the full context of salary data in the field of data science.

This treemap serves as an insightful visualization tool that categorizes various job titles within the data science field, enabling us to grasp the diversity and demand of roles available. By organizing these roles into a visually accessible format, the treemap allows us to observe how different job titles are distributed across the industry, as well as their relative prominence and frequency.

The treemap is not just a method of organizing data visually; it serves as a strategic tool that sheds light on the structure of the data science job market, enabling users to explore specific subfields and roles within the industry. By presenting a clear and accessible overview of various job titles, it allows individuals to identify and understand the nuances of different specializations, particularly emphasizing the diversity of roles and the corresponding compensation levels.
